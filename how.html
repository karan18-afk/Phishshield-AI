The pipeline

How PhishShield AI works

From the moment you paste a link to the moment you see a verdict, PhishShield AI follows the same four-step pipeline — entirely inside your browser.

01

URL input

You enter a link into the scanner. If you leave off http:// or https://, PhishShield AI adds http:// automatically so it can be parsed and analyzed.

02

Local analysis

The URL is broken down into its parts — protocol, hostname, path, query string — and run through 14 independent checks covering domain structure, encryption, link patterns, and page-path keywords.

03

Risk scoring

Every check that triggers adds weighted points to a 0–100 risk score. Stronger phishing indicators (like a hidden "@" redirect or a spoofed brand name) carry more weight than smaller signals (like a long URL).

04

Plain-language report

The final score maps to a verdict — Likely Safe, Suspicious, or High Risk — and you get a full report showing exactly which checks were flagged and which ones passed.

What we check, in detail

Every check below is independently testable and weighted. Nothing is hidden — this is the complete list PhishShield AI runs on every scan.

Domain & Hosting

  • Raw IP address used instead of a domain name
  • Punycode ("xn--") domains that can disguise look-alike characters
  • A known brand name on a domain that brand doesn't own — including look-alikes like "amaz0n" or "paypa1"
  • Top-level domains often used for free/disposable sites
  • Unusually deep subdomain chains

Connection Security

  • A hidden "@" used to disguise the real destination
  • Missing HTTPS encryption
  • Unusual port numbers in the URL
  • Known link-shortening services

Structure & Pattern

  • Multiple hyphens in the domain name
  • Unusually long URLs
  • Redirect patterns hidden in the URL path
  • An unusual number of digits in the domain

Content Signals

  • Words like "login", "verify", "secure" or "update" in the domain, path, or query

Understanding your score

Every triggered check adds points to the score, capped at 100. The total maps directly to one of three verdicts.

  • 0–19 · Likely Safe Few or no checks triggered.
  • 20–49 · Suspicious One strong signal or several minor ones.
  • 50–100 · High Risk Multiple strong signals together.
