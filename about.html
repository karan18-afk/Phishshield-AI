About this project

About PhishShield AI

PhishShield AI is a lightweight, browser-based tool that helps people spot the warning signs of a phishing link before they click it. It was built as a cybersecurity internship project with one goal in mind: transparency. Every result comes with a plain-language explanation of exactly which checks passed and which didn't — so a score is never a black box.

Our mission

Phishing remains one of the most common ways attackers steal credentials, payment details, and access to accounts — and most of it relies on links that look "almost right." Our mission is to make the patterns behind those links visible to everyone, not just security professionals, so people can pause and think before they click.

How it's built

The scanner runs entirely in your browser using 14 weighted heuristic checks grouped into four categories: domain & hosting, connection security, structure & pattern, and content signals. Each check contributes points toward an overall 0–100 risk score, which maps to one of three verdicts: Likely Safe, Suspicious, or High Risk. The How It Works page lists every single check.

Heuristic, not absolute. PhishShield AI is a rule-based educational tool, not a substitute for enterprise threat intelligence. A low score doesn't guarantee a site is safe, and a high score doesn't guarantee it's malicious — always verify through official channels before entering sensitive information.

Project context

This project was built as part of a cybersecurity internship to demonstrate front-end development, secure coding awareness, and a practical understanding of common phishing techniques such as domain spoofing, look-alike characters, and link shorteners.
